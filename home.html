About Our School

We are dedicated to providing our students with a comprehensive education in the field of medical science. Our faculty members are experts in their respective fields and are passionate about teaching the next generation of healthcare professionals.

Our Programs

  • Doctor of Medicine (MD)
  • Master of Science in Physician Assistant Studies (MPAS)
  • Master of Science in Nursing (MSN)
  • Bachelor of Science in Health Sciences (BSHS)
  • Doctor of Medicine (MD): This is the traditional four-year medical degree that qualifies graduates to practice medicine after completing residency training.
  • Doctor of Medicine (MD)
  • Doctor of Osteopathic Medicine (DO): Similar to an MD, but DOs receive additional training in a holistic approach to patient care, emphasizing the musculoskeletal system.
  • Doctor of Osteopathic Medicine (DO)
  • Physician Assistant (PA): PAs work under the supervision of physicians and can diagnose and treat a wide range of medical conditions. They typically require a master's degree. Physician Assistant (PA)
  • Nurse Practitioner (NP): NPs are advanced practice nurses who can diagnose and treat a wide range of medical conditions, prescribe medications, and order diagnostic tests. They typically require a master's degree.
  • Nurse Practitioner (NP)
  • Anesthesiologist: Anesthesiologists administer anesthesia to patients before, during, and after surgery. They require an MD or DO degree followed by residency training in anesthesiology.
  • Cardiologist: Cardiologists diagnose and treat diseases of the heart and blood vessels. They require an MD or DO degree followed by residency training in cardiology. Cardiologist
  • Dermatologist: Dermatologists diagnose and treat diseases of the skin, hair, and nails. They require an MD or DO degree followed by residency training in dermatology. Dermatologist
  • Emergency Physician: Emergency physicians provide care to patients in emergency room settings. They require an MD or DO degree followed by residency training in emergency medicine. Emergency Physician
  • Family Physician: Family physicians provide primary care to patients of all ages. They require an MD or DO degree followed by residency training in family medicine. Family Physician
  • Infectious Disease Physician: Infectious disease physicians diagnose and treat infectious diseases. They require an MD or DO degree followed by residency training in infectious disease. Infectious Disease Physician
  • Neurologist: Neurologists diagnose and treat diseases of the nervous system, including the brain, spinal cord, and nerves. They require an MD or DO degree followed by residency training in neurology. Neurologist
  • Obstetrician-Gynecologist (OB-GYN): OB-GYNs provide care to women throughout their lives, from pregnancy and childbirth to menopause. They require an MD or DO degree followed by residency training in obstetrics and gynecology. ObstetricianGynecologist (OBGYN)
  • Ophthalmologist: Ophthalmologists diagnose and treat diseases of the eye. They require an MD or DO degree followed by residency training in ophthalmology. Ophthalmologist
  • Orthopedic Surgeon: Orthopedic surgeons diagnose and treat diseases and injuries of the musculoskeletal system, including bones, joints, muscles, and ligaments. They require an MD or DO degree followed by residency training in orthopedic surgery. Orthopedic Surgeon
  • Pathologist: Pathologists examine tissues and body fluids to diagnose diseases. They require an MD or DO degree followed by residency training in pathology. Pathologist
  • Pediatrician: Pediatricians provide care to infants, children, and adolescents. They require an MD or DO degree followed by residency training in pediatrics. Pediatrician
  • Psychiatrist: Psychiatrists diagnose and treat mental health disorders. They require an MD or DO degree followed by residency training in psychiatry. Psychiatrist
  • Radiologist: Radiologists use imaging techniques such as X-rays, CT scans, and MRIs to diagnose diseases. They require an MD or DO degree followed by residency training in radiology. Radiologist
  • Surgeon: Surgeons perform operations to diagnose and treat diseases. There are many different types of surgeons, each specializing in a different part of the body. They require an MD or DO degree followed by residency training in surgery. Surgeon
  • Urologist: Urologists diagnose and treat diseases of the urinary tract, including the kidneys, bladder, and urethra. They require an MD or DO degree followed by residency training in urology. Urologist
